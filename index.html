Chapter 02 · Energy Flow

The 10% Rule
(why apex predators are rare)

Energy enters the ecosystem through the sun, gets captured by plants, and then leaks out as heat at every step up the food chain. By the time you reach the top, only a sliver remains. Click any tier of the pyramid.

⚡ The Ten Percent Rule

Only about 10% of energy at one trophic level passes up to the next. The other 90% becomes heat lost to the environment, gets used for movement, growth, or stays in waste. That's why the pyramid has to be a pyramid — there literally isn't enough energy to support more apex predators.

Chapter 06 · Invasive Species

The new arrivals
that ruin everything.

An invasive species is a non-native organism that, once introduced, harms its new environment — usually by out-competing native species, lowering carrying capacity, and unbalancing the food web. The fix? Three flavors of control.

⚠

What makes an invasive species so dangerous?

They have no natural predators in the new ecosystem, so their population grows uncontrolled. They outcompete native species for food, water, and space — driving down the carrying capacity for everyone else. Native species get crowded out or pushed to extinction.

Method 1
✋

Physical Control

Humans physically remove the invasive species — by hand, with tools, or with traps. Slow but precise.

Examples
  • Volunteers pulling kudzu vines
  • Scuba divers spearing lionfish
  • Trapping feral hogs
Method 2
⚗

Chemical Control

Pesticides, herbicides, or other chemicals are applied to kill the invasive species. Fast, but can harm non-target species.

Examples
  • Herbicides on crested floating heart
  • Insecticides on emerald ash borer
  • Aquatic herbicides on hydrilla
Method 3
🐟

Biological Control

Use a living organism — usually a natural predator or competitor — to control the invasive. Long-lasting if it works, risky if the helper itself becomes invasive.

Examples
  • Sterile grass carp eating hydrilla
  • Ladybugs released to eat aphids
  • Cane toads (a famous failure!)

⚘ Three control types — one trick to remember

Physical = you do it. Chemical = chemicals do it. Biological = a creature does it.

The Last-Minute Cheat Sheet

Five minutes before the test. Read this once.

Levels of Organization

Organism → Population → Community → Ecosystem → Biome. Population = same species. Community = different species together. Ecosystem = community + abiotic factors (water, sunlight, soil).

The Energy Pyramid

Bottom (Producer) has the MOST energy. Each level up loses 90%. Only ~10% transfers. That's why apex predators are rare.

Food Chain vs Web

Chain: single line. Web: overlapping chains. Arrows point in the direction of energy flow — from prey to predator.

Producers vs Consumers

Producers make their own food (plants, sunlight). Primary consumers eat producers. Secondary eat primary. Tertiary eat secondary. Decomposers break down the dead.

Population Words

Immigrate = IN. Emigrate = OUT. Carrying capacity = max sustainable population. Limiting factors = anything that caps growth.

Invasive Controls

Physical = humans remove (pulling, spearing). Chemical = sprays/herbicides. Biological = a living predator/competitor.
